Television energy consumption in Australia

Using the Australian TV energy dataset, we explore which screen types, sizes and brands are most common — and how they relate to energy use and star ratings.

1. Screen technologies on the market

Australians can currently buy LED, , LCD, and OLED. LED TVs dominate, while OLED and LCD make up smaller but growing shares.

2. What screen sizes are most common?

Most TVs sold in Australia fall between 16–40 inches. Large screens above 100 inch are available but far less frequent.

3. Which brands have the most models?

Samsung, KOGAN, and LG lead the market with the greatest number of models. Smaller brands have fewer options.

4. Power consumption by technology

Different screen technologies vary in efficiency. LED models tend to use less power per year than OLED, which consume the most.

5. How does screen size affect power use?

Larger TVs consistently consume more electricity. The relationship is roughly linear: as the screen grows, so does energy use.

6. Star rating and screen size

Star ratings remain relatively consistent across screen sizes. This shows that efficiency depends more on technology and design than on screen size alone.

Tips to reduce TV energy use

  • Look for higher star ratings when buying a new TV.
  • Enable energy-saving or ambient light modes.
  • Reduce brightness for night-time viewing.
  • Turn off at the wall to avoid standby power draw.
